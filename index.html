Why I built this

I have three kids.
They needed to learn this.

I'm a dad of three. Cash allowance always felt off — the dollars got misplaced, ended up in the laundry, or just disappeared into a drawer. Chores felt like a punishment they'd been sentenced to. Stickers got ignored. Lectures bounced off.

So one weekend I started building. The model is simple, and it's the one we already use at home: some chores are paid work. Some are family chores — this is how we help each other, no money attached. And kids can propose their own jobs with a price tag — that's how they learn to spot work worth doing, and parents approve it.

Then I added the part I really cared about — an optional way for them to watch their savings grow in Bitcoin. Not a wallet. Not a custody product. Just a window onto the future of money, set up by parents.

My three kids use it every day. Other parents kept asking, so it's on the App Store — free forever for the first 1,000 families. No ads. We don't store your data, so we couldn't sell it if we tried.

Why this matters

In a 25-year longitudinal study at the University of Minnesota, researcher Marty Rossmann found that the best predictor of young adults' success in their mid-twenties was whether they began doing household tasks at ages three or four — even after controlling for socioeconomic status, family structure, and education.

FAQ

The honest answers.

Is it free? +

It's free forever for the first 1,000 families on the App Store. No in-app purchases, no subscriptions, no ads. After that, we may charge a small one-time fee to cover hosting — but anyone who downloaded under the early-family window stays free, forever.

Do kids need their own device? +

Nope. They can use their own or share yours. Each family member has their own profile, so balances and progress stay personal.

How do kids actually get paid? +

The app doesn't move dollars — it just tracks completed jobs you can mark paid. Coinkids doesn't hold balances or touch your bank.

How we do it at our house: each kid has an Apple Cash account on their device. Chore gets done, we send the dollars there, mark it paid in Coinkids. When grandparents drop a birthday card with $20, the cash gets handed over and goes straight to Apple Cash too — so the money doesn't end up in the laundry, on the counter, or in some random drawer.

Now they can see their money and pay at the store themselves. That last part matters more than I expected. When it's their money disappearing at checkout, they finally feel what things actually cost.

Other families use cash, Venmo Teen, Greenlight, or an envelope on the fridge. Whatever works for you.

Is the Bitcoin stuff mandatory? +

No. It's completely optional — you choose during setup, and you can flip it off in Settings any time. When it's on, kids can see the balance of a wallet you've funded and watch it grow. Coinkids is not a wallet — it can't send, receive, or store Bitcoin. It just reads public chain data so your family can watch savings grow together.

How does the Bitcoin savings part work? +

We teach our kids to save a percentage of their earnings in Bitcoin. The mechanic is simple: they send us the cash from their wallet via Apple Pay, we buy the Bitcoin and send it to their wallet, and they watch it grow in the Coinkids app over time. Parents are the custody layer. Coinkids just shows the window.

What ages is it for? +

Designed for families with kids ages 6–12, but it works for any age. Listed in the App Store Kids Category (ages 6–8).

Is my family's data safe? +

Yes. We collect only first names. No emails, no passwords, no tracking. Data is encrypted and stored in your family's private cloud space. You can delete everything anytime from Settings. Full details in our Privacy Policy.
